this is a random idea that I had whilst observing how difficult it is for me to share my work sometimes. I get insecure, second guess myself and overthink. When thinking of potential novel uses for LLMs/AI this feels interesting, because it offers a different angle for how we might use this technology to help us in learning processes.
### Things to consider: 

**Voice and framing** -- how different should the bot's public voice be from mine?   should it be legible as a bot, or present as me?

**Trigger logic** -- does it respond to every note change, only new notes, only notes marked in a particular way? 

**What "shareable" means** -- this is the threshold question that's genuinely hard to encode. Is it about completeness, about a idea being sufficiently formed, about it connecting to your broader research arc?

**The approval UI** -- what information do I need to see to make a good ratification decision quickly? Just the proposed post and screenshot, or more context about why the bot chose this note?
